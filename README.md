# Motion Hologram
Here, we proposed a novel CGH technique called Motion Hologram to break the spatial coherence of light source, which can thus render photorealistic and speckle-free 3D scenes by jointly designing the holographic system using reinforcement learning. We reappraised the role of system motion, which is commonly believed detrimental in imaging and display, and conceptualized that the inherent speckles in holography could be neutralized and photorealistic 3D display could be achieved by spatially multiplexing the holograms via system motion.  Specifically, a novel system design paradigm that leverages reinforcement learning to jointly generate the phase-only hologram and system’s motion trajectory were developed, which enabled an unparalleled viewing experience. To showcase the superior performance of the proposed technique, we conducted numerical simulations and experimental reconstructions alongside traditional and time-multiplexed holography methods. We developed corresponding holographic benchtop prototype and demonstrated the proposed method achieved a 4-5 dB improvement in the PSNR of focal stacks compared to traditional holography, and is comparable to time-multiplexed holography. Furthermore, our method is capable of accurately reproducing full-color and high-fidelity multi-plane images with realistic defocus in visual effects. We believe our proposed method promises a new form of holographic displays that offers photorealistic and immersive experiences for audiences and provides a novel blueprint for computational design in various research areas.
